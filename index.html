Essential Indian Road Safety Measures

Mandatory Seatbelt Usage

As per Section 194B of MV Act, not wearing seatbelts attracts ₹1,000 fine. Front seat passengers must wear seatbelts in all vehicles equipped with them.

Law: Rule 138(3) CMVR - Mandatory for driver and front seat passenger

No Mobile Phones While Driving

Using mobile phones while driving is prohibited under Section 184 of MV Act with ₹5,000 fine for first offense. Hands-free devices are also discouraged.

Law: Rule 3A MVR - ₹1,000 fine for first offense

Strict Drink & Drive Laws

Section 185 mandates ₹10,000 fine or imprisonment up to 6 months for first offense (blood alcohol >30mg/100ml). Second offense within 3 years leads to ₹15,000 fine and 2 years imprisonment.

Law: Section 185 MV Act - 0.03% BAC limit

Traffic Signal Compliance

Jumping red light attracts ₹5,000 fine under Section 184. Disobeying traffic directions (Section 119) can lead to ₹2,000 fine.

Law: Section 122 MV Act - ₹1,000-2,000 fine

Pedestrian Rights & Safety

Drivers must yield to pedestrians at zebra crossings. Not giving way attracts ₹500 fine. Pedestrians should use footpaths and cross only at designated points.

Law: Section 138 CMVR - Pedestrian right of way

Two-Wheeler Safety Rules

Helmets are mandatory for both rider and pillion under Section 129. Violation attracts ₹1,000 fine and license suspension for 3 months. Only ISI-marked helmets permitted.

Law: Rule 138 CMVR - Mandatory helmet rule

Document Requirements

Must carry: Driving License (DL), Registration Certificate (RC), Insurance, PUC Certificate, and Fitness Certificate (for commercial vehicles). Missing documents attract ₹500-2,000 fines.

Law: Section 130 MV Act - Documents to be carried

Child Safety Norms

Children below 4 years must use child restraint systems. Those under 14 cannot sit in front seat. Violation attracts ₹1,000 fine under Section 194C.

Law: Section 194C MV Act - Child safety rules

Speed Limits in India

National Highways: 100km/h (cars), 80km/h (bikes). City limits: 50-70km/h. School zones: 25km/h. Exceeding limits attracts ₹1,000-2,000 fine under Section 183.

Law: Section 112 MV Act - Speed regulation

Key Indian Traffic Laws & Penalties

Driving Without License

₹5,000 fine or imprisonment up to 3 months (Section 181)

Overloading Vehicles

₹20,000 + ₹2,000 per extra tonne (Section 194)

Dangerous Driving

₹1,000-5,000 fine and/or imprisonment up to 6 months (Section 184)

No Pollution Certificate

₹10,000 fine for first offense (Section 190(2))

Unauthorized Vehicle Modifications

₹5,000 fine (Section 52 read with Rule 126 CMVR)

Triple Riding on Two-Wheelers

₹2,000 fine and license suspension for 3 months (Section 128)
